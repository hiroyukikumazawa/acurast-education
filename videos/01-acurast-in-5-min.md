## Goal

Explain what Acurast is, who it’s for, and what you can do next (provider or developer path).

## Primary references (official)

- `https://docs.acurast.com/`
- `https://docs.acurast.com/acurast-processors/`
- `https://hub.acurast.com/`

## Scene-by-scene script

### Scene 1 — Hook (0:00–0:30)

- **On-screen**: “Decentralized compute, powered by phones.”
- **Voiceover**: “What if the world’s most abundant computers — smartphones — could run real workloads securely, without relying on a few data centers? That’s the promise of Acurast.”

### Scene 2 — The ecosystem (0:30–2:00)

- **On-screen**: Roles: Providers → Developers → Users
- **Voiceover**: “Acurast connects three groups: compute providers who run the processor app, developers who deploy applications, and users who benefit from open, verifiable compute.”

### Scene 3 — Lite vs Core (2:00–3:15)

- **On-screen**: “Processor Lite (everyday phone)” / “Processor Core (dedicated Android)”
- **Voiceover**: “You can provide compute using Processor Lite on your everyday phone, or Processor Core on a dedicated Android device that’s factory reset and locked down for maximum reliability.”
- **B‑roll**: Screenshots from the official processor overview.

### Scene 4 — Hub (3:15–4:15)

- **On-screen**: `hub.acurast.com`
- **Voiceover**: “The Acurast Hub is the web console where you onboard devices and interact with the ecosystem.”

### Scene 5 — What to do next (4:15–end)

- **On-screen**: “Choose your path”
  - “Become a provider”
  - “Deploy your first app”
- **Voiceover**: “If you want to contribute compute, follow the official provider onboarding guide. If you want to build, start with ‘Deploy your first app’ using the Acurast CLI.”
