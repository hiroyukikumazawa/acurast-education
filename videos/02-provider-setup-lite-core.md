## Goal

Help a new compute provider choose Lite vs Core and successfully onboard.

## Primary references (official)

- Processor overview: `https://docs.acurast.com/acurast-processors/`
- Become a compute provider: `https://docs.acurast.com/processors/become-compute-provider/`
- Hub: `https://hub.acurast.com/`

## Script

### Scene 1 — Why provide compute (0:00–0:45)

- **On-screen**: “You can power decentralized compute with a phone.”
- **Voiceover**: “Acurast lets you provide compute using a smartphone. In this video we’ll set up either Processor Lite or Processor Core and connect to the Hub.”

### Scene 2 — Lite vs Core decision (0:45–2:30)

- **On-screen**: Side-by-side comparison
- **Voiceover**:
  - “Lite runs on your everyday phone, no factory reset.”
  - “Core is Android-only and uses a dedicated, factory-reset, locked-down phone for maximum reliability.”

### Scene 3 — Requirements checklist (2:30–3:30)

- **On-screen**: Android 12+, iOS 15+ (iPhone 6S+), locked bootloader, non-rooted
- **Voiceover**: “Before you start, confirm your device meets the official requirements.”

### Scene 4 — Onboard in Hub (3:30–7:30)

- **B‑roll**: `hub.acurast.com` onboarding UI + phone app connection flow
- **Voiceover**: “Open the Hub and follow the official onboarding steps for your processor type.”

### Scene 5 — Best practices (7:30–end)

- **On-screen**: Power + Wi‑Fi + uptime
- **Voiceover**: “For better reliability, keep the phone charging, prefer stable Wi‑Fi, and for Core use a dedicated device.”
